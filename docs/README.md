# Audrix

Real-time local intelligence engine for Windows. Captures microphone or system audio for speech-to-text, and reads or analyzes any screen region with local OCR and Vision. Everything stays on your machine.

## What it does

Audrix listens to what's playing on your PC and turns it into text in real time, while also letting you read and analyze anything on your screen with local OCR and Vision. Transcribe your own voice, a YouTube video, a Zoom call, or both at once. Or select any screen region to extract text or get visual analysis. No recording step, no waiting, no cloud upload.

- **Mic + System Audio** — Capture your microphone, any app's audio output, or both simultaneously with automatic speaker separation.
- **Noise Suppression** — Configurable noise gate and auto-gain keep background hum and silence out of your transcripts.
- **Crosstalk Detection** — Dual-stream mode distinguishes the local user from remote speakers on calls.
- **Multiple Backends** — Plug in Whisper, Lemonade NPU acceleration (AMD Ryzen), or any external transcription endpoint. Switch at runtime.
- **Screen OCR** — Select any region of your screen and extract text instantly with local optical character recognition.
- **Vision Analysis** — AI-powered visual understanding of on-screen content, diagrams, and UI elements, all running offline.
- **100% Offline** — All processing happens on your machine. Your audio and screen data never leave.

## Getting started

Download the latest release from GitHub and run `Audrix_Community.exe`.

No installation, no dependencies, no account required.

## Requirements

- Windows 10 / 11 (64-bit)
- 4 GB RAM (8 GB recommended for larger models)
- Optional: AMD Ryzen 7040+ with XDNA NPU for hardware-accelerated transcription

## Community edition

A free community build is available. Session duration is capped at 30 minutes with cooldown periods between uses. Studio and Enterprise editions are available for extended use.
